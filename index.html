What are Crypto Currencies?

A cryptocurrency is a digital or virtual currency that is secured by cryptography, which makes it nearly impossible to counterfeit or double-spend. Many cryptocurrencies are decentralized networks based on blockchain technology—a distributed ledger enforced by a disparate network of computers.
Some popular cryptocurrencies are:

  • Bitcoin (BTC) - Most popular cryptocurrency invented in 2008.
  • Ethereum (ETH) - Second largest cryptocurrency launched in 2015.
  • Dogedcoin (DODGE) - peer-to-peer coin introduced in 2018.

What is Forex?

The foreign exchange market is a global decentralized market for the trading of currencies and determines foreign exchange rates for every currency. It includes all aspects of buying, selling and exchanging currencies at current or determined prices. Currencies like:

  • United States dollar (USD) - The officail currency in the US
  • British Pound Sterling (GPB) - The offical currency in the UK.
  • EURO (EUR) the official currency of 19 of the 27 member states of the European Union.
  • Australian Dollar (AUD) - The currency of Australia.
